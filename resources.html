The Menstrual Cycle Explained

The menstrual cycle is a natural process that occurs in women of reproductive age. It typically lasts between 21-35 days, with the average being 28 days. For women with PCOD, cycles may be irregular or absent, which is a key symptom of the condition.

Menstrual Cycle Phases

  1. Menstrual Phase (Days 1-5): The shedding of the uterine lining
  2. Follicular Phase (Days 1-13): Development of follicles in the ovary
  3. Ovulation (Day 14): Release of an egg from the ovary
  4. Luteal Phase (Days 15-28): Preparation for possible pregnancy

PCOD & Your Cycle

In PCOD, hormonal imbalances can cause:

  • Irregular periods (oligomenorrhea)
  • Absent periods (amenorrhea)
  • Heavy bleeding
  • Prolonged periods
  • Unpredictable cycles

Did you know? Women with PCOD often have higher levels of androgens (male hormones), which can disrupt the regular menstrual cycle by preventing follicles from maturing properly.

Understanding Period Products

Choosing the right period products can make managing menstruation more comfortable, especially for women with PCOD who may experience irregular or heavy bleeding. Here's a comprehensive guide to available options:

MYTH: Women with PCOD shouldn't use tampons or menstrual cups.

FACT: There's no medical reason why women with PCOD cannot use internal period products. The choice of period product should be based on personal comfort, lifestyle, and flow rather than having PCOD. However, if you experience unusual pain or discomfort, consult with your healthcare provider.

Managing Irregular Periods with PCOD

Irregular periods are common in women with PCOD due to hormonal imbalances. Here are some strategies to help manage unpredictable cycles:

  1. Track your symptoms: Use the PCOD Wellness Hub's tracking feature to monitor your cycle, even if irregular.
  2. Maintain a healthy weight: Even a 5-10% reduction in body weight can help regulate periods in many women with PCOD.
  3. Regular exercise: Moderate physical activity can help balance hormones and improve insulin sensitivity.
  4. Healthy diet: Focus on anti-inflammatory foods, complex carbohydrates, and foods with a low glycemic index.
  5. Stress management: Chronic stress can worsen hormonal imbalances; practices like yoga, meditation, or counseling can help.
  6. Hormonal treatments: Your doctor may recommend hormonal birth control to regulate your cycle.
  7. Always be prepared: Keep period products with you at all times due to unpredictability.

Important: If you haven't had a period for more than 3 months (and you're not pregnant), experience extremely heavy bleeding, or have severe pain, consult with your healthcare provider promptly.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is it normal to have very irregular periods with PCOD? +

Yes, irregular periods are one of the most common symptoms of PCOD, affecting about 80% of women with the condition. This happens because the hormonal imbalances associated with PCOD can prevent regular ovulation, leading to unpredictable or missed periods. However, with proper management through lifestyle changes, nutrition, exercise, and sometimes medication, many women can achieve more regular cycles.

Can I get pregnant if I have irregular periods due to PCOD? +

Yes, women with PCOD can still get pregnant, though it may be more challenging due to irregular ovulation. Many women with PCOD successfully conceive, either naturally or with fertility treatments. If you're trying to conceive, work with a reproductive endocrinologist who specializes in PCOD. They can help track your ovulation and recommend appropriate treatments to improve your chances of pregnancy.

What's the best way to track my irregular periods with PCOD? +

Use a comprehensive tracking app like PCOD Wellness Hub that allows you to record not just period dates but also symptoms, mood changes, and physical signs.
